What Is ROAS?

ROAS — Return on Ad Spend — measures how much revenue you earn for every dollar you spend on advertising. It's one of the most direct measures of ad campaign profitability and is used across Google Ads, Meta Ads, and any paid channel.

Formula: ROAS = Total Revenue Generated ÷ Total Ad Spend

A ROAS of 4x means you earned $4 in revenue for every $1 you spent on ads. In Australia, a ROAS of 4x or above is generally considered good across most industries, though this benchmark varies significantly by sector and campaign type.

A ROAS below 2x typically signals the campaign is not covering its costs once margins are factored in — unless you're in a high-margin industry or investing in brand-building campaigns with delayed returns. A ROAS between 2x and 4x is average: profitable for some business models, marginal for others depending on gross margin.

If your ROAS is below benchmark, the usual culprits are poor audience targeting, weak ad creative, or a landing page that isn't converting. All three are fixable — and improving one is often enough to shift ROAS significantly.
