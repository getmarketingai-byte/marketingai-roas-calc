What Is ROAS?

ROAS stands for Return on Ad Spend. It measures how much revenue you earn for every dollar you spend on advertising. It's one of the most important metrics for evaluating whether your ad campaigns are actually making you money.

ROAS = Revenue Generated ÷ Ad Spend

For example, if you spent $2,000 on Google Ads and generated $8,000 in sales, your ROAS is 4x — meaning you earned $4 for every $1 spent.

In Australia, a ROAS of 4x or higher is generally considered good across most industries. A ROAS between 2x and 4x is average — your ads are profitable but there's room to optimise. Below 2x, you may be spending more than you're getting back once margins and operating costs are factored in.

It's important to note that a "good" ROAS depends on your gross margins. A business with 80% margins can sustain a lower ROAS than one operating at 20% margins. Use ROAS as a directional signal, not the only measure of campaign health.

If your ROAS is consistently below 2x, it's worth reviewing your targeting, ad creative, landing page conversion rate, and whether your offer is well-matched to your audience. These are all fixable with the right system in place.
